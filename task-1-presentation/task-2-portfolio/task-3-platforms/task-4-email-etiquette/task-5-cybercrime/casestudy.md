# UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into approving fake payment requests. A typical case involves a college student who receives a phone call from someone pretending to be a bank official. The caller claims that the student’s bank account needs urgent verification. The fraudster then sends a UPI collect request and tells the victim that approving it will help receive money.

The student, unaware of how UPI requests work, approves the request. Instead of receiving money, the amount gets debited from their account. In some cases, attackers also ask victims to scan fake QR codes or share OTPs. Once the victim shares details, multiple transactions are performed quickly.

College students and new digital payment users are often targeted because they rely heavily on UPI apps and may not understand security risks. Fraudsters create panic by saying the account will be blocked, forcing victims to act quickly.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering funds. Victims may also face misuse of personal information. Reporting delays reduce chances of recovery. This case highlights the importance of verifying payment requests and never approving unknown UPI transactions.
